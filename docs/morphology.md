# Species Morphology Profiles

Botanically-grounded reference for parameterizing each species (branching/geometry) and
writing Codex `$imagegen` texture prompts (bark, foliage). Cross-checked across USDA
FEIS/Silvics, Virginia Tech Dendrology, NC State Extension, Morton Arboretum, Missouri
Botanical Garden, NPS, Lady Bird Johnson Wildflower Center, and desert-flora references.

Bark/stem sections include ready-to-paste image-prompt phrasing in _italics/quotes_.

## PART A — Maryland / North American Trees

### 1. White Oak — *Quercus alba*
- **Form:** 80–100 ft (24–30 m), forest to ~135 ft; trunk 3–4 ft dia; spread ~80 ft (≈ height). Pyramidal young → broad, rounded, irregular, wide-spreading. Alternate leaves; **decurrent** (no leader). Squat, muscular, silvery.
- **Branching:** Trunk divides low into heavy scaffold limbs. **Massive near-horizontal low limbs (~60–90° from vertical)**, thick, gnarled, contorted. High density, crooked secondaries → rugged "old-tree" character.
- **Bark:** Pale ashy-gray to whitish, almost silvery; scaly upper → irregular blocky/platy lower; shallow furrows, flat narrow vertical plates with flaky lifting edges. Prompt: *"pale ashy-gray to whitish oak bark, loose overlapping flaky scales and flat narrow vertical plates, shallow furrows, blocky lifting edges, weathered silvery texture."*
- **Foliage:** Obovate, **5–9 rounded lobes, NO bristle tips**, deep sinuses; 4–9 in. Dull green/blue-green above, pale glaucous below. Fall wine-red/purplish-brown, often marcescent. Dense terminal rosette-like clusters.
- **Signature:** Rounded bristle-less lobes; pale flaky bark; horizontal low limbs; wider-than-tall crown.

### 2. Northern Red Oak — *Quercus rubra*
- **Form:** 60–90 ft (18–27 m); spread 60–75 ft. Round, broadly rounded, symmetrical — cleaner than white oak. Alternate; excurrent young → decurrent with age; straight bole in forest.
- **Branching:** Stout branches at near-right angles (~70–90°), relatively straight (vs white oak gnarl); medium-high density, even rounded canopy; keeps central stem longer.
- **Bark:** Dark gray/gray-brown with signature **smooth shiny pale-gray stripes down ridge centers ("ski tracks")**; wide flat-topped ridges, shallow furrows. Prompt: *"dark gray oak bark with wide flat-topped vertical ridges separated by shallow furrows, smooth shiny pale-gray stripes running down the center of each ridge like ski tracks, continuous vertical striping."*
- **Foliage:** Oblong, **7–11 bristle-tipped lobes**, sinuses 1/3–1/2 to midrib; 5–8 in. Dark green above. Fall russet-to-bright-red, reliable. Even medium-coarse dark canopy.
- **Signature:** Bristle-tipped lobes; shiny vertical striped bark; round symmetrical crown; saucer-cap acorns.

### 3. Red Maple — *Acer rubrum*
- **Form:** 40–70 ft (12–21 m); spread 30–50 ft. Pyramidal/oval → rounded/oval, ascending branches. **OPPOSITE** leaves/branches. Fast, neat, dense.
- **Branching:** Opposite pairs → symmetrical mirrored splits (procedural rule: every split = mirrored pair). Branches ascend ~30–60° then spread; medium-fine density, slender twigs; single leader young, codominant stems common.
- **Bark:** Young smooth silvery-gray (beech-like); mature darker gray-brown in long narrow vertical scaly plates/ridges, shaggy lifting edges. Prompt: *"maple bark, young smooth silvery-gray, mature dark gray with long narrow vertical scaly plates and ridges, peeling/shaggy lifting plate edges."* Twigs/buds red & glossy.
- **Foliage:** Palmate, **3–5 (usually 3) triangular toothed lobes**, shallow V-sinuses; 2–6 in. Opposite. Dark green above, whitened/glaucous below. Fall brilliant reliable scarlet-to-red.
- **Signature:** Red across all seasons (flowers, samaras, twigs, fall); opposite branching; small 3-lobed toothed leaves.

### 4. Tulip Poplar — *Liriodendron tulipifera*
- **Form:** Open 70–90 ft; **forest 100–150 ft (30–46 m)**, historic to ~200 ft. Alternate; strongly **excurrent → ramrod-straight trunk, ~66% clear bole**.
- **Branching:** Dominant persistent leader; self-prunes lower laterals; upper branches ascend into a modest oval crown in the top third. Brittle wood. **Keep lower ~½–⅔ of trunk bare.**
- **Bark:** Young smooth light gray-green; mature **flat-topped interlacing ridges + pale whitish diamond/net furrows**, gray-brown. Prompt: *"gray-brown tree bark with tight interlacing flat-topped ridges and pale whitish diamond-shaped furrows, moderately deep, net-like criss-cross pattern, white-gray in the furrow crevices."*
- **Foliage:** Unmistakable **4-lobed palmate, near-square outline, notched/truncated flat-top apex** (tulip/cat-face). 3–8 in, long petioles → airy. Tulip flowers ~2–2.5 in, 6 yellow-green petals w/ orange basal band, borne high. Fall clear golden-yellow. Build cards around large individually-readable leaves.
- **Signature:** Straight clear bole + high small crown; 4-lobed flat-top leaf; orange-banded tulip flower; diamond-net bark.

### 5. Loblolly Pine — *Pinus taeda*
- **Form:** 60–100+ ft (18–30 m), to ~150 ft. Loosely pyramidal young → dense oval/rounded crown carried high; straight excurrent leader. "Lollipop-on-a-pole" — bare straight column + rounded tufted crown.
- **Branching:** **Whorled branches** (~1 whorl/yr, tiered); strong self-pruning → long clear trunk, live crown at top. Upper branches upswept, lower droop then shed.
- **Bark:** Young red-brown scaly; mature **thick ridged/furrowed with large flat scaly reddish-brown plates, chocolate-brown beneath flakes**. Prompt: *"thick reddish-brown pine bark with large irregular flat scaly plates separated by deep dark furrows, plates flaking in papery layers, chocolate-brown wood revealed beneath, blocky broken-plate pattern."*
- **Foliage:** Needles **in fascicles of 3, 6–9 in**, dark yellow-green, stiff, twisted. Cones ovoid-cylindrical 3–6 in, each scale a sharp prickle. Dense radial tufts/pom-poms at branch tips. Cards: fan/star clusters of 3-needle bundles.
- **Signature:** Bare trunk + high tufted crown; needles in 3s; prickly cones; reddish scaly-plate bark.

### 6. Eastern Redcedar — *Juniperus virginiana*
- **Form:** 30–50 ft (9–15 m). Dense pyramidal/conical → narrowly columnar-to-ovoid (crown:height ≈ 1:3). Evergreen, dense twiggy, often foliaged to ground.
- **Branching:** Numerous fine dense ascending twigs in scale foliage → solid opaque silhouette; branches persist low. High twig density, short internodes, narrow crown.
- **Bark:** **Reddish-brown/gray-brown, thin, fibrous, shredding in long vertical strips**, stringy/papery, weathering ashy gray. NOT furrowed/plated. Prompt: *"thin reddish-brown fibrous cedar bark peeling in long vertical shredding strips, stringy papery texture, ashy silver-gray patches where weathered, no deep furrows."*
- **Foliage:** Two types — adult scale-like ~1.5 mm appressed on 4-sided twigs; juvenile awl/needle ~6 mm sharp spreading. Winter bronzing. **Frosty-blue glaucous berry-cones ~¼ in** on females (dioecious). Cards: small overlapping fan-sprays; scatter frosty-blue berries.
- **Signature:** Narrow columnar evergreen to the ground; fibrous shredding bark; frosty-blue berries; dual foliage; winter bronze.

### 7. American Sycamore — *Platanus occidentalis*
- **Form:** 75–100 ft (23–30 m), to 140+; **largest-diameter eastern hardwood trunk, 3–8 ft dbh** (record ~13 ft); spread ≈ height. Broad, open, irregular, spreading, decurrent; often forks near base. Colossal, ghostly-pale in winter.
- **Branching:** A few very large crooked gnarled wide-spreading primaries that twist/zigzag; wide angles, ascend then arch to near-horizontal. Open coarse density. **Zigzag stout orange-brown twigs, NO terminal bud**; ring-like leaf scar. Model high angular irregularity.
- **Bark (signature):** **Camouflage exfoliating patchwork** — thin outer bark flakes in irregular jigsaw patches of olive-green/khaki-tan/gray-green/warm-brown over **smooth chalky creamy-white inner bark**. Vertical gradient: dark furrowed scaly reddish-gray base → mottled camo middle → smooth creamy-white upper limbs (ghostly white from afar). Prompt: *"exfoliating camouflage bark of American sycamore: smooth chalky creamy-white and pale gray inner bark exposed in irregular jigsaw patches, surrounded by peeling flakes of olive-green, khaki tan, and warm reddish-brown outer bark; mottled patchwork mosaic; upper trunk and limbs almost pure white, lower trunk darker, thick, deeply furrowed and scaly reddish-gray."*
- **Foliage:** Large maple-like **palmate 3–5 lobed**, as wide as long, coarsely toothed, 4–10 in wide (among largest simple eastern leaves). Fall dull yellow-brown. Cards: large individual palmate silhouettes in loose sparse clumps showing pale branches.
- **Signature:** White camo exfoliating bark; massiveness; crooked gnarled limbs; single ~1 in hanging "buttonball" per stalk; zigzag twigs.

### 8. Flowering Dogwood — *Cornus florida*
- **Form:** Understory **15–30 ft (4.6–9 m)**; crown low-branching, broadly rounded, distinctly **FLAT-TOPPED**, often wider than tall; short trunk, often multi-stem. Opposite; decurrent. Wide flat-topped tiered silhouette.
- **Branching:** From a short trunk, branches grow **strongly horizontally in stacked tiers/layers** (stratified, shelf-like). Opposite buds → paired branching; fine terminal twigs turn up at ends. Flowers/foliage on upper surfaces of tiers → floating flat shelves. Twigs slender green-purple glaucous; clove/turban gray flower buds.
- **Bark:** Classic **"alligator hide"** — smooth gray young → mature dark gray-brown/blackish in a tight mosaic of small square/rectangular scaly blocks. Prompt: *"mature flowering dogwood bark: dark gray-brown to charcoal-black, broken into a tight patchwork of small square and rectangular scaly blocks — 'alligator hide' texture; blocky plated fissured pattern, each block raised and slightly rounded, separated by narrow crevices."*
- **Foliage:** **Opposite oval/ovate**, pointed, entire, 3–6 in, **arcuate venation** (6–7 vein pairs curving forward — render this). Fall brilliant red to reddish-purple. Spring: **4 large white (or pink) petal-like bracts, ~2 in, notched tips**, display 3–4 in facing up on tiers. Cards: flat leaf-sheets in horizontal planes; overlay 4-bract flowers on tier tops; red-purple in fall.
- **Signature:** Horizontal tiered flat-top; 4-bract flowers; alligator bark; clusters of 3–5 scarlet drupes at tips; small stature.

## PART B — Desert / Arid Plants

### 9. Saguaro — *Carnegiea gigantea*
- **Form:** Giant single-stem columnar tree cactus. 30–40 ft (9–12 m), range 10–52 ft. Stem to ~30 in dia. Extremely slow (35 yr to first flower; 150–175+ yr life). **Age legible in form:** young = single spear, old = many-armed candelabra. Make age↔height a tunable, precip-dependent curve.
- **Growth:** One massive vertical **ribbed column** (unbranched most of life). **Arms** appear only in maturity (~50–70 yr wet / 75–100 yr dry; some never), starting when trunk ~10 ft; count 0 to 25+, exceptional 50+, record 78. **Arm geometry:** buds laterally → grows out & down (droops) → re-orients **vertically upward parallel to trunk** = "U"/candelabra ("more a Y than a T"). **12–30 vertical woody ribs** with accordion pleats. Params: 1 trunk; arm probability ∝ age/height; arm = short out-down then long up segment; rib count drives cross-section.
- **Surface/spines:** Smooth waxy **blue-green/gray-green** skin, deep vertical accordion fluting up trunk & arms; corky gray-brown at old base. **Areoles every ~1 in along rib crests** → crisp evenly-spaced vertical dotted rows, each ~30 gray-brown spines (to 2 in). Prompt: *"waxy blue-green ribbed saguaro skin, deep vertical accordion pleats, evenly spaced rows of gray-brown radiating spine clusters along rib crests, matte desert sheen, gray corky bark at base."*
- **Foliage:** **No leaves.** White waxy night flowers 3.4–4.9 in in an apical ring at trunk/arm tips (Apr–Jun); ruby fruit 2.5–3.5 in. Only sprites: apical flower ring, apical fruit cluster.
- **Signature:** Tall ribbed blue-green column; candelabra of upswept U-arms; evenly spaced spine rows on accordion ribs; apical flowers/fruit.

### 10. Joshua Tree — *Yucca brevifolia*
- **Form:** Arborescent yucca, **irregular shaggy angular candelabra** — stout trunk repeatedly forking into crooked arms each tipped by a spiky **rosette ball of dagger leaves** (Seussian). 20–40 ft (to 70). Slow (~5.9 cm/yr); to ~300–1000 yr. No annual rings.
- **Growth:** **Dichotomous/pseudo-dichotomous forking:** unbranched shaft + terminal rosette until a **trigger** (flowering-induced terminal-bud death, or weevil damage) → splits into **2 (sometimes more) diverging crooked branches**, each re-capped by a rosette. First fork 3–9 ft; branches 7–20 ft, re-fork every ~2–3 ft → erratic zig-zag candelabra. Params: recursive ~2 children/fork, **stochastic trigger** (not regular), wide irregular angles (30–60°+), internode 0.6–0.9 m, rosette per terminal. Heavily randomize angles.
- **Surface/bark:** Tips cloaked in dense **spiral of stiff leaves** + **skirt of downward dead brown-gray leaves** below live rosette. Older trunk: rough **packed dead leaf-base bark, reddish-brown/gray, deeply checked/corky** (plates 3–6 in), spiral leaf-scars. Prompt: *"rugged Joshua tree bark, reddish-brown to gray, made of packed dead leaf bases, deeply checked corky texture, shaggy skirt of dead brown leaves below spiky green rosette, spiral leaf-scar pattern."*
- **Foliage:** Dense terminal **rosettes** of stiff **bayonet/dagger leaves** in a spherical spiky pom-pom. Leaves 15–35 cm × 0.7–1.5 cm, sharp terminal spine; dark blue-green/gray-green with **white finely serrated margins**. Creamy erect flower panicles 20–55 cm. Card: hemispherical/spherical burst of ~50–100 stiff blue-green blades w/ pale serrated edges; one rosette per tip.
- **Signature:** Shaggy angular unpredictable forking; spiky leaf-ball rosettes at every tip; dead-leaf skirts; checked leaf-base bark. Erratic asymmetry is the identity.

### 11. Cholla — *Cylindropuntia bigelovii* (teddy bear; + genus)
- **Form:** Short dense shrubby cactus from a **chain of stubby cylindrical segments**. Teddy bear: dark **trunk** + rounded crown of pale densely-spined joints that **glow gold/silver backlit**. 1–5 ft. Branches short, near-horizontal, crowded at top → rounded mushroom/lollipop crown; old plants ringed by dropped segments. Genus: cylindrical jointed stems (vs Opuntia's flat pads) + papery spine sheaths.
- **Growth:** **Chain of cylindrical joints** budding end-to-end and off sides. Each joint ~4–18 cm × ~1–2+ cm (teddy bear shorter/stouter); **tuberculate** (bumpy knob areoles). **Detachable joints** (jumping cholla) barb into fur, root where they fall → clonal colonies. Trunk darkens to brown/black vs pale crown. Params: chained capsules ~5–15 cm × 2–4 cm budding at small irregular angles, crowded near top; short dark trunk; scatter detached segments on ground.
- **Surface/spines (money shot):** Each areole ~6–8 spines (4–12), 1–1.8 cm, so dense they **hide the stem** — continuous fuzzy pelt. Spines **silvery-white/golden in papery translucent detachable sheaths → backlit golden/silver halo**; darken brown/black with age. Prompt: *"teddy bear cholla stem segment, cylindrical joint densely wrapped in silvery-golden translucent barbed spines, papery spine sheaths glowing backlit halo, fuzzy plush appearance, bumpy tuberculate green stem beneath, dark blackened woody trunk at base."*
- **Foliage:** Leaves reduced/absent; green cylinder photosynthesizes. Modest yellow-green blooms (~1.4 in) at joint tips. Card: the spine-wrapped segment IS the sprite (fuzzy backlit gold cylinder); instance segments, not leaf cards.
- **Signature:** Glowing fuzzy plush golden spine halo (backlighting essential); jointed tuberculate sausage-link chain; dark trunk vs pale crown; ring of detached joints.

### 12. Soaptree Yucca — *Yucca elata* (+ general yucca rosette)
- **Form:** Arborescent yucca: slender woody trunk (single or forking into a few candelabra arms), each capped by a **dense spherical/hemispherical rosette of stiff radiating sword-leaves**; tall bare flower stalk in summer. 9–15 ft (to 30). Very slow (~1 in/yr). Young = trunkless basal rosette like coarse bunchgrass.
- **Growth:** Brown cylindrical small-dia "pole" trunk, single or clumped 0.5–2.5 m. **Forks with age up to ~7 branches**, each with its own rosette head. **Rosette:** leaves in a tight phyllotactic spiral whorl at apex, radiating into a near-spherical ball — outer/lower blades droop, central up-and-out. ~50–100+ blades/head (modeling estimate). A 1–2 m flower stalk per rosette center. Params: 1–7 rosette-tipped arms, branch prob ∝ height, divergence ~15–40° off vertical. Shared yucca body plan.
- **Surface/bark:** Shaggy **"petticoat" of dead downward-curved brown-gray leaves** cladding trunk (persist 3–5 yr); below, gray fibrous checked/furrowed bark. Prompt: *"dried, downward-hanging gray-brown grass-skirt of dead yucca leaves over a slender gray fibrous trunk."*
- **Foliage:** Blades narrow stiff **linear-sword/needle, 25–95 cm × 0.2–1.3 cm**, sharp tip; gray-green/blue-green glaucous; **white edges shredding into curly white threads (filiferous)** — diagnostic. Tall creamy panicle above rosette. Card: radial fans of stiff blades (blue-green center, white fibrous edges, needle tip); separate tall bell-flower panicle sprite.
- **Signature:** Spiky spherical rosette(s); shaggy petticoat trunk; tall white flower spike; candelabra (up to ~7 heads); curling white marginal fibers.

### 13. Ocotillo — *Fouquieria splendens* (bonus)
- **Form:** Spreading **fountain/inverted-cone of many long straight whip-like thorny canes** from a short basal crown (not a cactus). 10–20 ft (3–6 m), ≈ as wide as tall. "Gray thorny fishing-rods, red-tipped in spring."
- **Growth:** Branches profusely at base then very sparingly — canes essentially **unbranched whips** (occasional tip fork). Cane count 6–100 (mode ~40–75), length 2–6 m, base Ø ~5–6 cm tapering to whip-thin. Splay in a funnel: center near-vertical, outer ~30–60° out, gentle outward curve (tips curve up). Params: sample cane base directions on a shallow cone; each cane a slightly curved tapering near-unbranched spline.
- **Surface/spines:** Canes gray/gray-green, **waxy resinous over longitudinal ridges/grooves**; living greenish/pliable, dormant gray-brown "dead sticks." **Stout sharp straight thorns to ~1.5 in along ridges** (hardened persistent petioles). Prompt: *"gray-green waxy ridged wand densely studded with stiff sharp gray thorns in spiral rows."*
- **Foliage:** Small oval/spoon bright-green leaves ~2–4 cm in spine axils; **drought-deciduous, cycles 4–5×/yr** (toggleable density driven by "rain"). **Terminal crimson/flame red-orange tubular flower panicles at cane tips** (Mar–Jun). Cards: (a) small green ovals along canes, (b) flame flower cluster at each apex.
- **Signature:** Fountain of straight thorny wands from a tiny crown; flame flower tips; dual state (lush green vs gray dead-sticks); thick-base/thin-tip taper.

### 14. Prickly Pear — *Opuntia* (bonus)
- **Form:** Low spreading shrubby **clump of flattened oval paddle pads (cladodes) chained edge-to-edge**, essentially no woody trunk. Genus 3–20 ft tall × 3–15 ft wide. *O. engelmannii* dense clumps to 3.5 m; *O. humifusa* ground mat ~6–12 in.
- **Growth:** **Chain-of-pads:** new pads bud from areoles along the **upper edge of older pads at varying angles** → branching chain (each pad a node). Params: from an old pad's top/edge areoles spawn 1–4 child pads, each rotated ±30–60° in/out of plane to fill volume; terminal *engelmannii* pads orient E–W to sun; pads pile/lean, mounding; oldest basal pads semi-woody. Pad sizes 10–40 cm × to 23 cm × ~2 cm.
- **Surface/spines:** Pads waxy **blue-green** glaucous (purpling under stress), regular **polka-dot grid of areoles**. **Two-part armament:** long spines (to 3 in, spreading down like a bird's-foot) **+ glochids** (tufts of tiny barbed bristles — the hazard). Prompt: *"waxy blue-green paddle, evenly dotted with areoles, each bearing a spreading bird's-foot cluster of pale yellow-white spines plus a fuzzy tuft of tiny barbed glochids."*
- **Foliage:** Pads ARE the foliage. Showy cup flowers 2–3 in (yellow/gold/orange/red/purple) on pad top edges; egg-shaped red-to-purple fruit ("tunas") on rims. Card: pad is base geometry (thick lens/paddle mesh, not a card); add instanced areole+spine clusters, edge flowers, edge tunas.
- **Signature:** Paddle pads chained edge-to-edge at varied angles (no trunk); areole polka-dot grid; dual armament; rim flowers; red-purple tunas.

### Confidence notes
- Saguaro arm onset is climate-dependent (50–70 yr wet / 75–100 yr dry) — treat as a range keyed to ~10 ft trunk height.
- Soaptree-yucca per-rosette leaf count (~50–100+) is a modeling estimate, not published.
- Two most design-critical texture callouts among trees: **sycamore white camo exfoliating bark** and **red oak shiny vertical "ski-track" striping**; among desert species: **cholla backlit golden spine halo** and **saguaro evenly-spaced spine rows on accordion ribs**.
